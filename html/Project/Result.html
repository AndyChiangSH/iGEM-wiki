Result

Overview

The primary goal of our project is achieving circular agriculture by reusing rice straws. Decomposing agricultural waste produced by the rice becomes the first aim. To fulfill the aim, we transferred the cellulase gene into Bacillus subtilis and used this modified bacteria to degrade rice straws.

Cellulase Plasmid Extraction

On the gel electrophoresis diagram, the third well contains scaffolding protein cipA . From seventh well to twelfth well, they respectively contain celA, K, R, S, and xyn C and Z.(Figure 1.)

These cellulosomes can be classified into three groups. CelA and celR are endo-glucosidases. CelK and celS are exo-glucosidases. XynC and xynZ are both xylanase. In order to achieve the maximum efficiency, the cellulosomes that have better performance in each group are chosen for the experiments, including xynC, celK, and celA.

Sugar concentration quantification

To quantify the sugar concentration in our broth, we mixed the DNS with the sugar that will change color while reaching one hundred celsius degrees. After calibration and testing of the OD600 value, we obtained the concentration of the sugar in the rice straw by analyzing how much the sugar generates, and calculated the weight of sugar generated from rice straw by concentration.

Our cellulosomes are assauredly viable because ensurement is made that our experimental environment is sterile and only the cellulosomes we put in can decompose the cellulose and hemicellulose.(Figure 2.)

The next step is to transfer the pyrroloquinoline quinone(PQQ) gene into another chassis-Bacillus, which can make the chassis-Bacillus endophytic and produce pyrroloquinoline quinone to accelerate rice growth.

Extract PQQ plasmid from Bacillus subtilis WB800N

Gene Expression On Bacillus subtilis WB800N

To test the effectiveness of PQQ on plants, we inoculate bacillus subtilis WB800 within or without the PQQ plasmid into Arabidopsis.

In the picture below, respectively shows phenotypes of 5-week-old Arabidopsis inoculated with Bacillus subtilis WB800 with PQQ plasmid(PQQ), with Bacillus subtilis WB800N(WB800N), and with water(CK) (Figure 4A)

Bacillus subtilis WB800N with PQQ plasmid significantly improved stalk height, the stalk height of Arabidopsis with PQQ is about 8 cm, and the stalk height of Arabidopsis with Bacillus subtilis WB800N is about 2 cm.(Figure 4B)

Arabidopsis inoculated with water has one primary inflorescence. Arabidopsis inoculated with Bacillus subtilis WB800N have 3 inflorescences, including primary and secondary inflorescence. Arabidopsis inoculated with Bacillus subtilis with PQQ plasmid have nearly 5.25 inflorescences, encompassing primary, secondary and tertiary inflorescence. (Figure 4C)

Bacillus subtilis WB800N with PQQ plasmid possibly help plants to reach reproductive stages earlier.

Arabidopsis inoculated with PQQ has the highest performance in the leaves area of Arabidopsis. The leaves area is about 5 cm², in contrast to 4 cm² of Bacillus subtilis WB800N, and to 3 cm² of CK (Figure 4D)

However, we discovered that WB800N has a kanamycin resistance gene in its genome. So we decided to make Bacillus subtilis RM125 as the new host.

Engineering success

Plasmid Extraction and PCR

We conducted plasmid extraction and PCR to verify that the PQQ plasmid was successfully transferred into Bacillus subtilis RM125(Figure 5. & 6.)

Growth Curve

The OD600 value of Bacillus with PQQ plasmid is higher than wild type Bacillus in log phase and stationary phase. PQQ can improve the efficiency of nutrient utilization of Bacillus subtilis.(Figure 7.)

Gene Expression On Bacillus subtilis RM125/PQQ

Inoculate Arabidopsis

Comparing with Arabidopsis which didn’t be inoculated (CK), the leaves area of Arabidopsis thaliana inoculated Bacillus subtilis RM125 with PQQ Plasmid(RM125/PQQ) increased by 1.5 times and capsule number raised by 2 times. Leaves NO. and stalk height also grew significantly. Bacillus subtilis RM125 with PQQ Plasmid can improve both vegetative and reproductive growth of Arabidopsis thaliana.(Figure 8B and 8C)

In addition, the value of leaf number, leaves area, stalk height, and capsule of Arabidopsis thaliana inoculated with our engineered bacteria B.subtilis RM125/PQQ(RM125/PQQ) is higher than the Arabidopsis inoculated with Bacillus subtilis RM125(RM125), and also more homogenous. (Figure 8E and 8F)

Inoculate Rice

Rice seeds were dehulled and sterilized, and constantly shaked in water under darkness for one day then sewed in Murashige & Skoog medium. Thirteen days after inoculation, we found that the value of leaves width, leaves length, shoot height of seedling inoculated B. subtilis RM125 with PQQ plasmid(RM125/PQQ) was higher than the seedling inoculated with B. subtilis RM125(RM125), and the fresh weight was doubled. (Figure 9.)

In test on Arabidopsis and rice, we got the same result: Bacillus RM125 inoculated with PQQ plasmid can effectively improve plant growth, and the PQQ gene may be the key factor.

Endophyte Re-isolation

Re-isolate Bacillus subtilis RM125/PQQ

After we had re-isolate Bacillus subtilis RM125 from Arabidopsis and checked it by sequence(picture 1), whether Bacillus subtilis RM125/PQQ was endosymbiotic would affect the persistence and effectiveness of the product. To check whether the Bacillus subtilis RM125/PQQ was endosymbiotic, re-isolating the plants inoculated with RM125/PQQ can be the most intuitive way. Then, filtration was done to the re-isolated strain by kanamycin, and then plasmid was extracted. When PCR and sequencing are done continuously, PQQ gene was discovered, which was the direct evidence that Bacillus subtilis RM125/PQQ is endosymbiotic.(Figure 10. & 11.)

Inoculate Supernatant of Bacillus subtilis RM125/PQQ

Verification of our assumption that PQQ was secreted off to the supernatant needed to be reassured. We decided to adjust the easiest way, observing the difference after the inoculation by supernatants. Supernatants from B. subtilis RM125 with PQQ plasmid(RM125/PQQ) and B. subtilis(RM125)

In this experiment, we pelleted down the cell and filtered the supernatant. The dilution fold of the supernatant is equal to the value of OD600 value because assumption was made that the amount of metabolites is proportional to the OD600 value.

Results showed that the supernatant of Bacillus improved the vegetative and reproductive growth of Arabidopsis, and the vegetative growth of Arabidopsis inoculated with our engineered Bacillus(RM125/PQQ) was slightly higher than Arabidopsis inoculated with Bacillus subtilis RM125 .(Figure 12.)

Proof of concept

Culture Bacillus subtilis RM125/PQQ with saccharified straw liquid

Our final purpose is to turn the agriculture waste to the medium of plant probiotics bacteria, and use the bacteria with the medium as a growth promoter to increase agricultural productivity. The final goal is to find the best carbon-nitrogen ratio of the saccharified straw liquid to culture Bacillus subtilis RM125 with PQQ plasmid. We have rice straw for carbon sources and Okara for nitrogen sources. After a series of experiments, we found that 1:4 is the best ratio of rice straw to Okara. (Figure 13.)The OD600 value of Bacillus subtilis RM125 with PQQ can achieve nearly twice or twice and a half increase in the saccharified straw liquid.(Figure 14.)

Rice Inoculation

To avoid Soil-Borne disease, seeds of rice（Oryza sativa var. Japonica Taiken 9) was sterilized by NaClO, sowed in potting soil, and inoculated three times (12 days,33 days,60 days after sowing_DAS).

For biosafety reasons, we filtered out the Bacillus, inoculated rice only with supernatant. After culturing our endophyte in saccharified straw liquid,we adjusted the OD value to 1，then pellet down the bacteria and filtered the supernatant as stock inoculant.The stock inoculant was diluted 100 times 200times and 500times.The concentration of the supernatant is defined as 1x, 1/100x,1/200x, and 1/500x.

Supernatants of higher concentration seemed to be more effective. (Picture 1 &2)Treatment with 1x and 100 x supernatant significantly increased the leaves number, shoot height, and leaf length.(Figure 15A,15D,15E)The tiller quantity of rice inoculated 1x supernatant escalated by 50.3%(Figure 15B) and the dry weight was doubled.(Figure 15C) In the meantime, the tiller quantity of rice inoculated 100x supernatant was raised by 29%(Figure 15 B), the dry weight was increased by 96%( Figure15C), and chlorophyll content raised 1.5 times(Figure 15F).

More tillers means the rice may have more ears ,and the increase of chlorophyll content means the increase of photoassimilates.All these phenomena pointed out that that inoculant can improve rice growth and have a great opportunity to increase the yield.

The vegetative phase of rice is about two months, and about 3-5 seedlings will be planted together until the total amount of tiller reaches 20-25, then the rice is ready to enter the reproductive phase. According to this, 4 tillers is the lowest value for every single plant. While the control groups have about 4 tillers, inoculated rice(1x,100x) have already reached 6-8 tillers,( Figure16B) which means that inoculants shorten the time for rice to reach 4 tillers, helping rice enter the reproductive phase earlier. Water sources are becoming a serious problem in agriculture because of global climate change. If the time for rice cultivation shortens, a lower amount of water is needed.

Besides, the culm diameter also increased, which means that the inoculants may have the potential to improve breaking-type lodging resistance. (Figure 16C)

Our rice has ears!

The treatment group is getting ears,and the experiment is still in progress.
