Description

Description

More than half of the world’s population feeds on rice as the staple food. The annual consumption of rice is about 500 million metric tons. Rice production is quite large. More than 90% of the world’s rice is grown in Asia. Rice consumption by Asians accounts for more than 90% of the world's total consumption. Rice is also regarded as a staple food in Taiwan, but the additional amount of rice stalks produced is quite large. Taking Taiwan’s rice as an example, the output of rice stalks in 2015 was 1.58 million metric tons. In fact, the method of disposal of rice stalks is not proper. In the past, rice stalks were often treated by burning in Taiwan, but these improper treatment methods caused many problems.

Due to the difficulty in collecting and transporting rice stalk, some farmers are accustomed to the simplest and quickest way to burn the rice stalks after harvest. In fact, the high temperature during combustion will destroy the microbiota of the topsoil, and the ash of rice stalks will cause too much potassium carbonate and calcium oxide to cause soil acidification and uneven fertility. In addition to the dense smoke that will affect pedestrians' sight and cause road safety, particulate pollutants will also pollute the air. Burning rice stalks will produce PAHs, CO2, PM2.5 and other greenhouse gases and greenhouse gas precursors, resulting in a greenhouse effect. It may interfere with the earth's heat balance and cause global climate abnormalities, affecting the living environment of human beings, and burning gas can directly affect people's health.

To solve the problem,we constructed the Clostridial cellulase genes within Bacillus subtilis WB800 and the cellulosomal enzymes could be formed. It will be used for effective straws decomposition.By doing so,we hope to establish a more sound system to protect the environment and people’s health.

After successfully decomposing the rice stalks, we found that if the rice stalks are only converted into fermentation broth and still discarded, we just convert one waste into another, which does not make the resources more efficient. In view of the results of NCHU_Taichung HP's interview with farmers in 2019, we found that climate change had already caused farmers to suffer great losses, so we began to think about whether we can help farmers overcome or reduce the losses caused by climate change. If the time and conditions required for the growth of crops can be controlled, crops can be prevented from being damaged by the weather when they are about to be harvested. In view of the above two points, we further converted the fermentation broth produced by the decomposition of rice stalks into a plant growth promoter and cooperated with the intelligent agricultural system to control and monitor the growth of crops. In this way our plan not only meets the public's need to avoid the troubles caused by burning rice stalks, but also benefits the farmers who work hard. We transferred the plastids that can make bacteria produce PQQ to Bacillus subtilis RM 125 by transgene and used the fermentation broth produced by the decomposition of rice stalks to culture Bacillus that can produce PQQ. Successfully made rice stalks become plant growth promoters. By collecting rice stalks from farmers and giving back plant growth promoters, a good cycle is established for farmers to build a smart agricultural system. In addition to moving towards the realization of circular agriculture, it also creates a win-win situation.
